Indoor Plant Fertilizing Calendar – Frequently Asked Questions

Updated: June 2025

How often should I fertilize my indoor plants?

Most houseplants benefit from feeding every 4–6 weeks during active growth in spring and summer. Over-fertilizing can burn roots, so always follow product instructions.

Do indoor plants need fertilizer in winter?

During winter, most plants enter a rest period. Reduce or stop fertilizing until early spring unless you have grow lights or tropical varieties.

What kind of fertilizer is best for houseplants?

Use a balanced liquid fertilizer like 10-10-10 or 20-20-20 diluted to half-strength. For flowering plants, a bloom booster may be appropriate.

Can I use organic fertilizer for my indoor plants?

Yes. Organic options like fish emulsion, worm castings, and seaweed extract are great alternatives, though they may have stronger smells.

Should I water before or after fertilizing?

Water first if your soil is dry. Fertilizing dry roots may damage them. Always apply fertilizer to moist soil for even absorption.
